Report a Contractual Breach

Submit a confidential breach report to Manchester LMC for investigation

All breach reports are treated in strict confidence. Submission does not constitute a formal complaint but enables the LMC to identify systemic issues and act collectively on behalf of Manchester practices.

The Manchester LMC uses breach reports to monitor the relationship between practices and the ICB, NHS Trusts, and other commissioning bodies. Where patterns emerge, we can escalate concerns collectively — protecting individual practices while addressing the underlying issue.

Guidance

What Should You Report?

The following categories cover the most common types of contractual breach. If your situation does not fit neatly into one of these, please use the "Other" option in the form below.

Report this when an ICB or commissioner imposes inappropriate referral limits that prevent you from referring patients in line with your clinical judgement. This includes blanket restrictions, prior-authorisation requirements not agreed in your contract, and targets that compromise patient safety.

  • Numerical caps on referrals to specific specialties
  • Requirements to obtain commissioner approval before referring
  • Financial penalties or informal pressure linked to referral rates
  • Mandated use of alternative pathways that do not meet patient needs

Report this when commissioners or NHS Trusts make changes to agreed care pathways without proper consultation with practices or the LMC. Practices are entitled to be consulted before pathway changes take effect.

  • Unilateral changes to discharge or follow-up protocols
  • Removal of secondary care services redirected to primary care without resource
  • New or amended pathways imposed without a consultation period
  • Changes that create additional workload without corresponding funding

Report this when your practice receives demands to share patient data that are inappropriate, lack a lawful basis, or fall outside agreed data sharing arrangements.

  • Requests for bulk patient data without a clear lawful basis
  • Pressure to provide data to commercial or third-party organisations
  • Requirements to use systems that extract data without explicit practice agreement
  • Requests that conflict with your Data Protection Officer's advice

Report this when the ICB or NHS England imposes unsafe workload requirements — including minimum appointment numbers, staffing ratios, or access targets — that create an unreasonable risk to patient safety or GP wellbeing.

  • Mandated minimum daily appointment numbers without resource support
  • Access targets (e.g. same-day quotas) imposed without funding
  • Requirements to accept patient lists beyond safe registered list capacity
  • Directives to operate with staffing levels you consider clinically unsafe

Report this when your practice has delivered agreed enhanced services but payment has been withheld, delayed without explanation, or disputed by the ICB on grounds not set out in the original service specification.

  • Non-payment beyond standard payment timescales
  • Clawback of previously paid enhanced service fees without due process
  • Retrospective changes to service specifications used to deny payment
  • Disputes over eligibility criteria not made clear at service sign-up

Process

What Happens Next?

  1. 1

    Acknowledgement

    You will receive an automated acknowledgement email within 24 hours. An LMC officer will review your report within three working days.

  2. 2

    Initial Assessment

    An LMC officer will assess whether the issue is isolated or part of a wider pattern. They may contact you for clarification using the email address provided.

  3. 3

    Collective Action

    Where a systemic issue is identified, the LMC raises it collectively with the relevant commissioner or NHS body. Individual reports are anonymised unless you have agreed otherwise.

  4. 4

    Feedback & Outcome

    We will update you on the outcome. Where the issue cannot be resolved collectively, we can advise on individual options including formal grievance and contractual dispute processes.

Need to speak to someone urgently?

If your situation requires immediate advice rather than a written report, please call us directly. Our officers are available Monday to Friday, 09:00–17:00.
