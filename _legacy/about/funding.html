How We're Funded

Manchester LMC is funded entirely by the GPs and practices it represents — with no government subsidy or commercial income.

Independent Funding, Independent Voice

As a statutory body under the National Health Service Act 2006, Manchester LMC must be financially independent of NHS England and ICBs. This independence is essential — it means we can represent GPs robustly, without fear or favour, even when that means challenging commissioners or government policy.

Our funding comes from two sources: a statutory levy on GP practices, and a voluntary subscription for locum and portfolio GPs. Both are modest, transparent, and set by the elected committee.

We do not receive grants, sponsorship, or income from pharmaceutical companies. We hold no commercial conflicts of interest.

At a Glance

  • Statutory levy on all NHS GP contracts in Manchester
  • £100/year voluntary subscription for locums and portfolio GPs
  • No commercial income, grants, or pharmaceutical funding
  • Accounts published annually at AGM; open to all members

The Practice Levy

Every GP practice holding an NHS contract in Manchester contributes to the LMC through a statutory levy — an obligation set by national legislation.

Who Pays

All practices holding a GMS, PMS, or APMS contract within the Manchester area. The levy is calculated in relation to the registered list size and the number of whole-time-equivalent GPs.

How It's Set

The levy rate is agreed each year by the elected LMC committee at the Annual General Meeting (AGM), where all member practices have a vote. Rates are set to cover operating costs only — we do not accumulate surpluses.

What It Funds

Staffing, committee meetings, representation at regional and national LMC conferences, legal and HR consultation, and the maintenance of guidance, news, and member services for all practices.

Locum & Portfolio GPs

Voluntary Subscription

Locum GPs, portfolio GPs, and those not working within a contracted practice are not covered by the practice levy — but they still have access to everything Manchester LMC offers, including confidential advisory services, wellbeing support, and access to our guidance library.

To support this, we offer a voluntary annual subscription of £100 per year. Subscribers receive full member benefits:

  • Access to the subscriber-only area (agenda papers, meeting minutes, open forum)
  • Confidential advisory support from the Managing Director
  • Representation at the LMC and at national conferences
  • Regular newsletters and updates
  • Access to all guidance documents and resources
  • Invitation to LMC meetings and events

Accountability & Transparency

We are accountable to our member practices. Our finances are reviewed annually and our committee is democratically elected.

Annual General Meeting

The AGM is held each spring and is open to all GP principal contractors in Manchester. At the AGM, members can:

  • Review and approve the annual accounts
  • Elect committee members for vacant seats
  • Vote on the levy rate for the coming year
  • Raise any matter of concern to general practice in Manchester
  • Receive reports from the Chair and Managing Director

AGM dates are published in our Events calendar and notified to all practices by letter and email.

Committee Elections

The LMC committee consists of elected GP representatives drawn from across the Manchester area. Seats are allocated to reflect the diversity of practice types and geographic sub-areas within our patch.

Elections are held in accordance with the Constitution. Any GP principal contractor within our area is eligible to stand for election. Committee members serve a fixed term and may stand for re-election.

Current committee members are listed on our Our Team page.

Our Constitution

Manchester LMC operates in accordance with a written constitution that sets out our statutory basis, the composition and election of the committee, meeting procedures, financial governance, and the rights of member practices.

The constitution was last updated in 2022 in line with changes to NHS organisational structures following the Health and Care Act 2022 and the formation of Greater Manchester Integrated Care Board.
