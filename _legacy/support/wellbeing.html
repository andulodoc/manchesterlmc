Wellbeing Hub

We care about the people who care for Manchester.

You give everything to your patients. We want to help you look after yourself.

GP burnout is not a personal failing — it is a system-wide challenge. Manchester's GPs and primary care staff are working under unprecedented pressure: rising demand, workforce shortages, complex patients, and relentless administrative burden. It takes a toll.

Manchester LMC is committed to supporting the mental health and wellbeing of every GP and practice staff member across our patch. This hub brings together the best available resources — NHS services, peer support, crisis lines, digital tools, and financial assistance — all in one place.

If you are struggling right now, please reach out. You do not have to manage alone, and everything you share with us remains confidential.

If you are in crisis right now, please call Samaritans on 116 123 (free, 24/7) or the Manchester Mental Health Crisis Line on 0800 953 0285. If there is an immediate risk to life, call 999.

Mental Health Support

Free, confidential services specifically for doctors and healthcare professionals.

NHS Practitioner Health

NHS Service — Free for doctors

A free, confidential NHS primary care service for doctors and dentists with mental illness or addiction problems. Offers therapy, psychiatry, and clinical support without it affecting your GMC registration by default.

Visit Website

BMA 24/7 Counselling

BMA — Free & Confidential

The BMA's counselling and peer support service is free for all doctors, medical students, and their immediate families. Trained counsellors are available around the clock — no appointment needed.

0330 123 1245 More info

Samaritans

Listening support — Free, 24/7

Samaritans provide confidential listening support to anyone in emotional distress or struggling to cope — including doctors. No judgement, no advice, just someone there to listen at any time of day or night.

116 123 More info

Peer Support & Mentoring

Connect with fellow doctors who understand the pressures of general practice.

GP Support (GP-S)

Peer support for GPs

GP-S is a peer support scheme run by GPs for GPs. Trained GP supporters offer one-to-one phone support on a voluntary basis. A confidential conversation with someone who truly understands the job.

Visit GP-S

BMA Peer Support Groups

BMA — Nationwide

The BMA runs structured peer support groups for doctors across the country, including specialty-specific groups. Groups meet regularly (usually online) to share experiences and strategies for managing the demands of medical practice.

Find a Group

NHS Leadership Academy Mentoring

NHS — Mentoring scheme

The NHS Leadership Academy runs a national mentoring scheme connecting healthcare professionals with experienced mentors. Structured mentoring conversations support career development, resilience, and leadership in primary care.

Apply for Mentoring

Crisis Support

Immediate help available around the clock. You do not have to face a crisis alone.

Manchester Mental Health Crisis Line

Local — Free, 24/7

Manchester's dedicated mental health crisis line connects you to a trained crisis worker any time of day or night. Available to anyone in Manchester experiencing a mental health crisis, including healthcare workers.

0800 953 0285

Papyrus HOPElineUK

Suicide prevention — Free

Papyrus HOPElineUK provides confidential support and advice to people concerned about their own or someone else's suicidal thoughts. Trained advisors can discuss safety planning and next steps.

0800 068 4141 More info

Shout Text Service

Text support — Free, 24/7

If you're struggling but don't feel able to talk, text SHOUT to 85258. Shout's trained crisis volunteers offer confidential text-based support for anyone in crisis — available any time, entirely free.

Text SHOUT to 85258
More info

Free Apps & Digital Tools

Evidence-based digital wellbeing tools — free or subsidised for NHS staff.

SilverCloud

Online CBT — Free via NHS

SilverCloud offers clinically validated, structured online cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) programmes for anxiety, depression, stress, and sleep difficulties. Available free to NHS staff — no GP referral required, available 24/7.

Access SilverCloud

Headspace

Mindfulness & meditation — Free for NHS

Headspace offers guided meditation, mindfulness, and sleep content to support mental health and resilience. The full app is available free for all NHS staff — register with your NHS email address to access the complete library.

Get Free Access

Unmind

Wellbeing platform — NHS staff

Unmind is a workplace mental health platform offering science-based tools to measure and improve wellbeing. Programmes cover topics including stress, anxiety, sleep, and resilience — designed specifically for busy professionals.

Access Unmind

Financial Assistance

Financial difficulty can happen to anyone. There is help available and no stigma in asking.

Cameron Fund

Financial assistance — Grants & loans

The Cameron Fund has provided financial assistance to GPs and their families since 1970. The Fund offers grants and interest-free loans to registered GPs in financial difficulty due to illness, mental health problems, family breakdown, or other hardship. The application process is means-tested and completely confidential — there is no contact with employers or the GMC.

Visit Cameron Fund

BMA Benevolent Fund

BMA — Financial support

The BMA Benevolent Fund provides financial assistance and welfare support to BMA members and their dependants who are facing hardship. This can include help with living costs, disability adaptations, or other one-off financial pressures. Applications are assessed with compassion and discretion.

Visit BMA Fund
