About Manchester LMC

Your statutory representative body for general practice across Manchester — established 1974

Our Role

What Is a Local Medical Committee?

A Local Medical Committee (LMC) is a statutory body established under the National Health Service Act. Every GP in England is represented by their local LMC, whether they are aware of it or not. The LMC is the official channel through which GPs engage with NHS England, ICBs, and local commissioning bodies.

Manchester LMC represents approximately 360 GPs across 90 practices in the Manchester area. We are independent of management structures and wholly committed to representing the interests of GPs and the patients they serve.

We are elected by the GPs we serve and funded through a statutory levy on NHS primary care contracts — meaning there is no subscription required for practice-based GPs and salaried GPs. Locum GPs can subscribe separately for £100 per year.

Our Work

What We Do

Manchester LMC works across four main areas to support and represent general practice.

Representation

We attend ICB meetings, NHS England forums, and joint negotiating committees on your behalf. We give GPs a collective voice in decisions that affect how general practice operates in Manchester.

Confidential Advice

Individual GPs can contact us in confidence about GMC investigations, NHS complaints, partnership disputes, CQC inspections, and employment matters. We are on your side.

Guidance & Resources

We produce, curate, and distribute practical guidance on clinical, contractual, legal, and regulatory matters — saving practices time and ensuring they have access to up-to-date information.

Wellbeing Support

We signpost GPs and practice staff to mental health, peer support, and financial assistance resources. GP wellbeing is not optional — it is essential to safe, high-quality patient care.

Our Area

Coverage Area

Our Story

A Brief History

1948

NHS Founded

The National Health Service Act 1946 comes into force. Local Medical Committees are established as the statutory representative bodies for GPs within the new NHS structure.

1974

Manchester LMC Established

Following NHS reorganisation, Manchester LMC is formally constituted in its current form to represent GPs across the Manchester area under the new Area Health Authority structure.

1990

GP Contract Reforms

The 1990 GP Contract introduces significant changes to general practice funding. Manchester LMC plays a key role in local implementation and in representing practices affected by the changes.

2004

New GMS Contract

Introduction of the new GMS contract and the Quality and Outcomes Framework. The LMC supports practices through transition and represents Manchester GPs in local negotiations.

2013

Clinical Commissioning Groups

NHS England and CCGs take over commissioning. Manchester LMC adapts its liaison structures to engage with the new commissioning landscape while maintaining GP independence.

2022

Integrated Care Systems

CCGs are replaced by Integrated Care Boards. Manchester LMC establishes new liaison arrangements with Greater Manchester ICB and continues to represent GPs in this evolving system.

Today

Looking Forward

Manchester LMC continues to evolve — expanding digital resources, the wellbeing hub, and collective action support — while maintaining its core mission of representing Manchester's GPs.

Our Place in the System

How We Relate to Other Bodies

BMA & GPC England

The British Medical Association's GP Committee negotiates the national GP contract. Manchester LMC feeds local intelligence to the GPC and implements national agreements locally. LMC representatives may stand for GPC election.

NHS England & ICB

Manchester LMC has a statutory right to be consulted by NHS England and Greater Manchester ICB on matters affecting general practice. We attend ICB primary care meetings, workforce groups, and commissioning panels.

Other LMCs

We work alongside neighbouring LMCs — including Salford, Stockport, and Bury — through the Greater Manchester LMC network to coordinate on regional issues. NAPC and the national LMC conference provide a broader forum.
